COUNTERPARTY RISK

Diversified and capped: no large idle balances

Venue diversification with per-venue caps, minimal idle balances on exchanges, optional custody for reserves, and a flexible counterparty setup so we are not tied to any single provider.

  • Exposure ladders by liquidity bucket (how fast we can exit) and stress-loss checkpoints.
  • Daily oversight; weekly IC (Investment Committee) for sizing/rotation.
  • Books are activated only when post-cost edge is positive; otherwise, they sit at zero.

Strategy books

Machine-learning long/short (perpetual futures vs. spot)

ML-driven long/short in liquid markets, refreshed daily to capture cross-sectional momentum (assets that recently out- or under-performed). Directional exposure can be taken when model confidence is high.

Basis, opportunistic

Cash-and-carry or funding capture only when yields are attractive, usually in a few bursts per year. When carry is thin, we stand down.

Gamma harvesting, options

Short-dated, hedged premium capture on top venues, sized small, with strict limits and quick adjustments.

Yield on idle cash

Conservative yield on USD/stablecoins via tokenised T-bills and custodial programs. Higher-risk yield (structured funding capture or secured lending) only when carry clearly exceeds costs and within hard limits.

ALLOCATION & RISK

How we deploy, rotate, and cut

  • Decisioning: daily PM oversight + weekly IC to size, rotate, or pause books.
  • Activation rule: expected profit after all costs (fees, funding/borrow, execution slippage) must be clearly positive.
  • Loss checkpoints: each book has pre-set loss triggers; portfolio-level limits sit on top.
  • VaR (Value-at-Risk): monthly 95% VaR < 10% as a portfolio design guardrail.

The ML long/short book is the anchor. We rotate into basis, options, and yield when pricing and liquidity are favourable.
